The Project

The Australasian Legal Information Institute (AustLII) provides a free access online database to Australasian legal material. This includes all criminal, common‐law, and commercial matter cases heard by the County Court of Victoria. Of particular interest is the sentencing document collection that forms the case base of precedence consulted by lawyers in constructing their arguments and by judges in informing their sentencing decisions. Sentencing law in Australia is a combination of statutory and common law. It entails hundreds of variables and objectives which impact on sentencing outcomes. Judges acknowledge that sentencing is the most complex and least rewarding aspect of their judicial role. Moreover, sentencing decision‐making is not a structured or stepwise process. Rather it is a discretionary and largely intuitive process whereby courts state the factors that they are taking into account and then prescribe a specific sanction. Given the inherent complexity involved in the decision‐making process, legal professionals face the difficult task of reliably retrieving and interpreting information. For this reason, this project seeks to develop a software solution, based on natural language processing (NLP), to automatically recognize legal arguments, properties, and relationships in order to “demystify” sentencing decision‐making and identify the considerations that actually influence sentence decision‐making. In the first stage, the project is concerned with a natural‐language processing tool chain that filters the relevant terms and encodes this information into a feature matrix (i.e., a data representation digestible by machine learning algorithms). Students have to design and implement the natural‐language processing tool chain, and, in consultation with the host supervisors, develop an automatic classification process that maps identified concepts to the legal aggravating and mitigating factors. In the second stage, the project will apply off‐the‐shelf statistical and probabilistic machine learning algorithms in order to identify the relevant factors and quantify their impact on the sentencing decision‐making process. For the purpose of interpretation by a legal expert, the results of this stage will be visualised in a standard Web browser.
